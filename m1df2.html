Discussion Forum 2 Summary Post

Introduction

Thanks to everybody who replied to the initial post. The first part of the initial post was about anti-virus software, while the second part was about the concept of “zero-trust” and micro-segmentation.

Discussion

Anti-virus can be very effective as the end-point protection against malware. For its best efficiency it requires the user to update it regularly. Stella has pointed out that Avast anti-virus software had severe privacy issues, which I was not aware of. Avast, who also acquired AVG anti-virus software were collecting and selling user’s personal data and clickstream data to third party customers, obtained via browser extension for several years (Martens, 2020). They are probably not the first company to prioritise profit over ethics. Similar to hackers, they kept collecting data until they got noticed and publicly exposed for their actions.

Zero-trust approach can reduce the cyber-attack zone to a specific radius, and hence limits the attackers to access a wider network area. It is often referred to as a ‘layered fortress within the fortress’ (Simpson and Foltz, 2021). Nabil said that “zero-trust verification steps are required in every access to systems”, which leads to Amy’s statement that with micro-segmentation, the security of the data centres can improve while the network performance stays unaffected. Stella mentioned that micro-segmentation can be “extremely effective for large organisations with many employees working in different locations”, and I agree with her statement that it is “ideal for modern organisations, and remote workers”. It is very efficient because it acts as a secure end-to-end communication protection, hence minimising the ‘man-in-the-middle’ type of attacks (Simpson, 2022).

Conclusions

Both topics brought excellent insight and pros and cons to each. They are efficient methods of protection against malicious attacks, considering they are regularly updated and set up correctly. They should be used alongside other computer and network protection solutions, such as firewalls, Intrusion Detection and Intrusion Prevention Systems, and Virtual Private Networks, amongst others.
