Security and Risk Management

3rd module

Summary of the learning outcomes

  • Differenctiate between qualitative and quantitative risk assessment
  • Carry out both, qualitative and quantitative risk assessment
  • Understand and create threat models for various scenarios
  • Create quantitative risk models
  • Design and explain Disaster Recovery strategies and solutions

  • Each section has a (1) Content (take-aways), (2) Individual contribution, (3) Reflections, and (4) Artefacts (which will open on a separate page)

Week 3: Introduction to Threat Modelling and Management

Content

    • Familiarisation with the Threat Modelling techniques: Process for Attack Simulation and Threat Analysis (PASTA) (the method, not the Italian dish!), STRIDE framework, DREAD risk assessment, Attack Trees (which were my favourite!), etc.
    • Threats vs. Vulnerabilities
    • Application of the individual Threat Modelling techniques vs. hybrid approach (i.e., if they can be used in a combination)

Individual contribution

    • Summary Post posted on Discussion Forum 1

Reflections

    • SRM Team 2 started meeting regularly
    • Attack Trees are very similar to Fault Trees in terms of "And" and "Or" gates, and the quantification behind them; both are using the "Top-down" approach, with the difference that the Attack Tree is using an intuitive thinking of how the attacker could compromise the system, whereas the Fault Trees approach is determining system faults, and causes to those system faults

Week 7: An Introduction to the Concepts of Quantitative Risk Modelling

Content

    • Familiarisation with the Monte Carlo Simulations (MCS) and Bayes theorem-based methods
    • Familiarisation with multi-criteria decision analysis techniques such as Analytic Hierarchy Process (AHP), Analytic Network Process (ANP) and Technique for Order of Preference by Similarity to Ideal Solution (TOPSIS)
    • Pros & cons of each technique

Individual contribution

    • MCS and TOPSIS were discussed in the SRM Team 2 as possible techniques for the final assignment
    • Initial Post was posted to Collaborative Learning Discussion 2

Reflections

    • It was interesting to hear that MCS can be used as a prediction to identify top threats in an organisation (and the monetary effects these can have)
    • In order to run the MCS sucessfully, the search for 'good' and 'suitable' data began!
    • 10+ hours of YouTube videos were watched on how to run the MCS sucessfully, and how to best interpret the MCS results (if the MCS worksheets had such options)

  • Week 7 artefacts

Reflective piece

This module had a changing pace, with a combination of weekly activities (i.e., regular reading), seminars (and the preparation for the seminars, which is captured throughout this e-portfolio), as well as working on two assignments in a team, with people I have never met or worked with before. The team dedication was absolutely fantastic, and we worked very well in a team - like a team of soldiers! The module provided me with an insigt to qualitative and quantitative risk assessments, diffrent threat modelling techniques, the legislative challenges aroudn risk modelling (especially with the compliance with different standards in different countries), as well as the principles and practical applications of DR strategies and the challenges around the DRaaS. It was a very comprehensive module, and definitely challenging in terms of trying to fit a lot in a limited space of the assignments, but was enjoyable in terms of the topics covered, and the team performance, which was an absolute pleasure.
