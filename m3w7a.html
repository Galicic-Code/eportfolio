Week 7 Artefact: Discussion Forum 2 Initial Post

Posted on 19 April 2023

CVSS

Common Vulnerability Scoring System (CVSS) is a standard for rating the severity of the Information Technology vulnerabilities, currently at v3 (Mell et al., 2022). Severity is based on an equation, to which specific "exploitability" and "impact" metrics (i.e., "Base score" metrics) must be added. The score can be obtained with an "acceptable deviation" (Mell et al., 2022).

CVSS criticism

Spring et al. (2021) challenged the CVSS by stating that it is not intended for "purpose and as a proxy for risk". They reviewed the CVSS formula and suggested several improvements.

Non-transparent formula

One of the issues are qualitative answers to eight "Base score" metrics are measured on a 0-10 scale, with unclear distance between the answers, an assumption that severe vulnerabilities are more important, and there is no evidence or justification for the formula (Spring et al., 2021). In addition, Dawes et al. (2022) state that poor metrics descriptions can lead to inaccurate scores. Also, the subjective scoring depends on the experience of the personsâ€™ previous experience of CVSS (Dawes et al., 2022). In terms of scalability, the 10-point scale could easily be changed to a 5- or 7- point scale, however the mean scores might show higher compared to a 10-point scale (Dawes, 2008). Larger data volume might contribute to better, less skewed results (Dawes, 2008).

CVSS alternatives

Spring et al. (2021) suggest new calculation algorithm for risk elements, studies on the practical use of the CVSS, and more robust scoring system which should provide transparent and justifiable results. However, several different variations of somewhat modified CVSS have already been applied, using Bayesian networks, Markov chain, Fuzzy system approach, amongst others (Costa et al., 2022).

Conclusion

CVSS is perhaps not an ideal method for assessing the severity of vulnerability, however, it seems that with the experimental approach by modifying some (or eventually all) variables mentioned above, it will be refined and improved in the future.
