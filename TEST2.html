Week 10 Activity

After reading Opara-Martins et al (2014) and Morrow et al. (2021), the following questions were answered:

1. What are some of the main vendor lock-in issues the authors identify? How would you mitigate them?

a) System incompatibility
b) Lack of interoperability with other (cloud) applications
c) Exclusive licencing
d) Limited data migration (portability)
e) lack of standards / standardisation
f) Data integration, e.g. APIs
g) Cost
h) Hardware limitations

2. What are some of the security concerns with the modern cloud? How can these be mitigated?

a) Legal - different juristictions
b) Legal - legal disputes
c) Legal - policies
d) Access rights (unauthorised access)
e) Data - encryption
f) Data - removal/storage
g) Data - back-ups
h) Incident response

References

Morrow, T., LaPiana, V., Faatz, D., Hueca, A. & Richmond, N. (2021) Cloud Security Best Practices Derived from Mission Thread Analysis. Carnegie-Mellon Univ Pittsburgh PA.

Opara-Martins, J., Sahandi, R., & Tian, F. (2014) Critical review of vendor lock-in and its impact on adoption of cloud computing. In International Conference on Information Society (i-Society 2014) (pp. 92-97). IEEE.

Posted on 7 April 2023 by D. Milward

Hi Matjaz

this is a really interesting and insightful summary post. I completely agree about the differences between SMEs and larger companies, and how many US companies (such as AirBnB and Uber) see themselves as disrupters. However, I do NOT agree with their self-classification. I believe these companies are merely marketing companies using a new medium - the Internet - to ply a well-tried and proven approach. It also seems that they are not as successful as they would like because many of the 'resources' they rely on to make their money are rebelling and in some cases are even claiming they are being exploited - although as yet there is limited evidence for this.

As you say I think the real disruption would be using digital transformation to create a new business model that doesn't just trade on existing tropes but instead replaces them with more efficient processes - in the same way that the invention of the spreadsheet created the business market for the home micro. A very interesting discussion.

Hi Doug,

I agree with the reference to the 'disrupters vs. good marketing companies'. Until the laws and regulations allow them to 'disrupt' other companies, they might be able to 'get away' with it. However, they were unable to do so in certain countries, which already had a 'strong' legislation. Not to mention the impact some of these 'disrupters' have on the worker's rights and pay systems, i.e., salaries and taxes. Digital transformation itself could and should be more efficient, thinking beyond the financial gains, and more in the benefit of the society, moderate digital progression, as well as organisations' success.
